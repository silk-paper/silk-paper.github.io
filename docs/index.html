In submission

Abstract

Motion in-betweening is a critical tool for animators to create believable animations, allowing for nuanced control over pose-level details in each key frame. In this work, we present a simple yet effective transformer-based framework, for synthesizing realistic motions for motion in-betweening tasks. We demonstrate that the choice of pose representation significantly impacts the quality of the synthesized motions. Our model, a standard transformer encoder, consistently matches or surpasses the existing learning-based solutions across all metrics on the LAFAN1 benchmark and in a user study. SILK generalizes better to longer sequences and significantly outperforms alternatives when extrapolating to an unseen number of in-betweening frames. We also address the lack of a consistent methodology for evaluating and comparing existing solutions within the realm of learning-based motion in-betweening by introducing a cross-validation setup for the LAFAN1 dataset.
